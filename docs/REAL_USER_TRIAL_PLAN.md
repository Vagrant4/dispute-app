# ClaimProof SG Real User Trial Plan

## Trial Target

DISPUTE gives each newly verified account a 3-day trial. Testers can use the full field workflow during the trial; after it ends, Apple App Store or Google Play subscription is required to export reports.

Tester mix:

- 5 freelancers
- 3 subcontractors
- 2 site supervisors

## Trial Goals

Validate whether the app supports real evidence and progress claim workflows before monetization:

- Time tracking through clock in/out and manual entries
- Photo evidence capture, notes, project linking, and optional GPS handling
- Backup and restore behavior before uninstalling, changing phone, or resetting data
- PDF and CSV progress claim report generation
- Native sharing of reports and exported records
- Local-only data warning clarity
- Evidence lock usefulness for dispute support
- Pay summary usefulness for checking expected claim amounts

## Onboarding Checklist

Before each tester starts:

- Explain that ClaimProof SG V1 stores records locally on the phone.
- Remind testers not to enter real FIN/NRIC unless they are comfortable doing so.
- Ask testers to use realistic but non-sensitive client, project, and worker names where possible.
- Show how to create a project before logging time or photo evidence.
- Show clock in/out, manual time entry, photo evidence, pay summary, report generation, sharing, backup, restore, and evidence lock.
- Ask testers to export or back up important records before uninstalling, changing phone, or clearing app data.
- Confirm account registration and subscription status use the DISPUTE server, while work records remain local and analytics/cloud sync are not enabled.

## Daily Usage Checklist

Each working day, testers should try to:

- Clock in and clock out for at least one project.
- Add or correct one manual time entry if the real workflow needs it.
- Capture at least one photo evidence record with a useful note.
- Deny GPS permission at least once if comfortable, then confirm evidence can still be saved.
- Check whether the pay summary matches their expected amount.
- Note any missing evidence detail that would help a claim or dispute.

## Weekly Usage Checklist

At the end of each week, testers should try to:

- Review project activity and time records for missing or incorrect details.
- Generate one PDF progress claim report.
- Generate one CSV progress claim report.
- Share or export at least one generated report through the phone's native share sheet.
- Export a backup and confirm the backup warning is clear.
- Lock at least one finalized evidence record and assess whether the lock behavior makes sense.
- Record any workflow that still needs paper, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, or screenshots outside ClaimProof SG.

## Feedback Questions

### Clock In/Out

- Was clock in/out fast enough for site use?
- Did the app make it clear which project was being tracked?
- What would make forgotten clock outs easier to correct?

### Manual Entries

- Were manual entries easy to add and edit?
- Did manual entries capture enough context to explain late or corrected records?
- What fields were missing for a claim or dispute?

### Photo Evidence

- Was it clear which project each photo belonged to?
- Were notes, timestamps, and optional location enough to explain the evidence?
- What photo workflow still required another app?

### GPS Permission Denial

- Was it clear that GPS is optional?
- Could testers still save useful photo evidence without coordinates?
- Did the app avoid implying GPS was proof of authenticity?

### Backup/Restore

- Was the local-only warning clear before testers entered important records?
- Could testers find the backup action when needed?
- What would make restore safer or more understandable?

### Report Generation/Share

- Did PDF and CSV reports include the evidence needed for progress claims?
- Did native sharing work for the tester's real sharing channel?
- What report details were missing for clients, main contractors, or supervisors?

### Pay Summary

- Did pay summary amounts match tester expectations?
- Were rates, hours, and totals understandable?
- What calculation or explanation was missing?

### Evidence Lock

- Did locking a finalized record increase trust in the evidence trail?
- Was the difference between draft, finalized, and locked clear?
- Did locking happen too early, too late, or at the right point?

### App Trust/Privacy

- Did testers understand that V1 has no cloud sync?
- Did testers feel comfortable with the app storing evidence locally?
- What privacy wording would make the app more trustworthy?

## Success Criteria

Proceed toward wider paid rollout only if:

- Most testers can complete daily time and photo evidence workflows without support.
- At least one freelancer, one subcontractor, and one site supervisor can generate and share a useful progress claim report.
- Testers understand local-only storage and the need to export or back up important records.
- Evidence lock is understood as useful or the required copy/flow changes are small.
- No critical evidence, report, backup, or privacy workflow gap is discovered.

## Stop/Go Criteria Before Wider Paid Rollout

Go:

- Evidence and report workflows are usable in the 3-day account trial.
- Backup/export warnings are understood before testers risk important records.
- Remaining gaps are copy tweaks, small UI improvements, or known post-V1 placeholders.

Stop:

- Testers cannot produce useful PDF/CSV claim reports from real workflows.
- Photo evidence lacks context needed for claim or dispute support.
- Backup/restore confusion risks record loss.
- Local-only storage is misunderstood as cloud sync.
- Evidence lock causes avoidable loss of needed edits or trust.
- Testers identify missing workflows that would materially affect payment claims.

## Data Privacy Reminder

ClaimProof SG V1 has no cloud sync. Records are stored locally on the device unless a tester exports, backs up, or shares them. Testers should not enter real FIN/NRIC unless they are comfortable. Important records should be exported or backed up before uninstalling the app, changing phone, clearing app data, or resetting the device.

## Known Limitations

- No CPF automation
- No MOM automation
- No employer approval workflow
- No cloud backup
- No cloud sync
- Report export requires an active trial or subscription
- Mobile billing uses Apple App Store or Google Play through RevenueCat; Stripe checkout is not used in the app
- No GPS verification
- No Work Permit tracking
- No AI OCR
- No AI photo analysis
